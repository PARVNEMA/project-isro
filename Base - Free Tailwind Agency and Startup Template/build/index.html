The solar system has one star, eight planets, five dwarf planets, at least 290 moons, more than 1.3 million asteroids, and about 3,900 comets.. Our solar system orbits the center of the galaxy at about 515,000 mph (828,000 kph). It takes about 230 million years to complete one orbit around the galactic center. What Lies Beyond Our Planet? Discover with Space Safari!🌌

Our home, the Milky Way galaxy, is a majestic spiral galaxy teeming with billions of stars, dust, and gas. Stretching over 100,000 light-years wide, it's a swirling disc with long arms reaching out into space. Our solar system resides within one of these arms, far from the crowded, energetic center. But the Milky Way isn't alone. Beyond our galactic neighborhood lies a vast and unimaginable number of galaxies, each with its own unique structure and story. These galaxies come in various shapes and sizes, from majestic spirals like our own to towering ellipticals and faint, wispy irregulars. The universe is brimming with these island universes, each a testament to the grand scale and wonder of the cosmos.
