1,400 light-years away lies Kepler-452b, a super-Earth in the habitable zone of a sun-like star. 60% larger than Earth, it's likely rocky with a thicker atmosphere. This "Earth 2.0" could potentially hold liquid water, a key ingredient for life. Scientists are eagerly studying it, hoping to unlock secrets about its atmosphere, moons, and the possibility of a protective magnetic field. While not an exact Earth twin, Kepler-452b fuels the fire in the search for habitable worlds beyond our solar system.
